### 1. Threats
**Definition:** Dialogue explicitly referencing Voldemort, Death Eaters, dangerous curses, death, threats to safety, or the broader war against evil.

**Rules for inclusion**
- Mentions of specific antagonists (Voldemort, You-Know-Who, Tom Riddle, Bellatrix).
- References to death, murder, killing, or "The Unforgivable Curses."
- Discussions of Azkaban, Dementors, or dark artifacts (e.g., the Dark Mark).
- Warnings of immediate mortal danger or doom.

**Rules for exclusion**
- General rule-breaking that results in detention (Hogwarts Academics).
- Scary creatures not explicitly tied to Dark Magic (Magical Creatures).
- Spells used for practice or utility (Magic).
- Expressions of fear where the focus is on the emotion rather than the threat (Social Dynamics).

**Edge cases**
- "He's going to kill us" (Hyperbole): If said by Ron about a difficult exam, it is **Hogwarts Academics** or **Social Dynamics**. If said while running from Death Eaters, it is **Threats**.
- "The Chamber of Secrets has been opened": This describes a threat, but if the focus is on solving the mystery, it might lean **Strategy**. However, usually, the threat to life takes precedence -> **Threats**.

**Examples from data**
- "The Dark Lord will return. And the boy will be in terrible danger when he does." (Dumbledore)
- "Lily and James put their faith in the wrong person. Rather like you, Severus." (Dumbledore)
- "They're not Ministry. They're Snatchers. They hunt Muggle-borns..." (Ron)

### 2. Hogwarts Academics
**Definition:** Posts focused on school life, coursework, exams, house points, faculty interactions, and institutional rules.

**Rules for inclusion**
- Mentions of classes, homework, essays, grades, or the library.
- Discussions about school rules, getting expelled, or detention.
- House points, the House Cup, or inter-house rivalry (excluding Quidditch matches if they are purely sport).
- Announcements made by Dumbledore regarding school governance.

**Rules for exclusion**
- Spells used in combat or dangerous situations (Threats).
- Strategy happening within the school that drive the plot (Strategy).
- Social events like the Yule Ball, unless discussing logistics (Social Dynamics).

**Edge cases**
- "Killed, or worse, expelled": Prioritizes the academic consequence -> **Hogwarts Academics**.
- Quidditch: If discussing the points/cup -> **Hogwarts Academics**. If discussing the mechanics of flying -> **Magic**.

**Examples from data**
- "I have a few start of term notices I wish to announce." (Dumbledore)
- "Look at you playing with your cards. Pathetic! Exams are coming." (Hermione)
- "You said she was making you do lines!" (Ron)

### 3. Magic
**Definition:** Statements concerning the mechanics of magic, specific spell incantations, potions, wands, or the function of magical artifacts.

**Rules for inclusion**
- Reciting specific spells (e.g., *Alohomora*, *Riddikulus*).
- Correcting pronunciation or wand movement.
- Explanations of how a magical object works (Time Turner, Deluminator).
- Discussions about potion ingredients or transformation rules.

**Rules for exclusion**
- Using magic to fight for one's life (Threats).
- Using magic to solve a riddle in a task (Strategy).
- Simple possession of an object without explaining it (e.g., "Pass the map").

**Edge cases**
- "Wand out, Harry": This is a command, but it explicitly references the tool of magic -> **Magic** (or **Immediate Action** depending on urgency).
- Patronus Charm: If discussing the memory needed to cast it -> **Magic**. If using it to repel a Dementor -> **Threats**.

**Examples from data**
- "It's Levioooosa, not Leviosaaaar." (Ron)
- "This is a Time Turner Harry." (Hermione)
- "Wand out, Harry." (Dumbledore)

### 4. Social Dynamics
**Definition:** Exchanges focused on emotional bonds, loyalty, conflict resolution, family dynamics, and expressions of feelings between characters.

**Rules for inclusion**
- Apologies, thanks, and expressions of trust or mistrust.
- Romantic tension, jealousy, or asking someone to a dance.
- Family discussions (Weasleys, parents).
- Comforting someone who is scared or upset.

**Rules for exclusion**
- Strategic planning for the adventure (Strategy).
- Purely informational questions (Immediate Action & Logistics).
- Academic stress (Hogwarts Academics).

**Edge cases**
- "I'm scared": If seeking comfort -> **Social Dynamics**. If identifying a threat -> **Threats**.
- "Trust me": If regarding a plan -> **Strategy**. If regarding the friendship -> **Social Dynamics**.

**Examples from data**
- "I would trust Severus Snape with my life, Harry." (Dumbledore)
- "Mum fancies him." (Ron)
- "No one was screaming, Harry." (Hermione - comforting/correcting perception)

### 5. Strategy
**Definition:** Conversations driving the central plot: solving mysteries, planning missions, and executing tactical movements related to the adventure.

**Rules for inclusion**
- Discussions about the Sorcerer's Stone, Chamber of Secrets, or Horcruxes.
- Deducing clues or solving riddles (like for the Triwizard Tournament).
- Logistics and Commands: "Go to the trapdoor," "Grab the Cup," or "We need to move now"
- Planning missions (using Polyjuice Potion to sneak in).
- Deducing the plot or connecting clues ("The heir of Slytherin").

**Rules for exclusion**
- General schoolwork (Hogwarts Academics).
- Random monsters encountered (Magical Creatures).
- Social Dynamics reactions to the burden of the quest (Social Dynamics).

**Edge cases**
- "The Chamber has been opened": Reports a plot event -> **Strategy**.

**Examples from data**
- "The truth. Tell them Hogwarts is no longer safe... The Chamber of Secrets has indeed been opened again." (Dumbledore)
- "We know about the Philosopher's Stone!" (Ron)
- "It was standing on a trap door... guarding something." (Hermione)

### 6. Magical Creatures

**Rules for inclusion**
- Mentions of Hagridâ€™s pets (Norbert, Aragog, Fang).
- Discussions about specific species (Hippogriffs, Dragons, Cornish Pixies).
- References to pets (Scabbers, Crookshanks, Hedwig).

**Rules for exclusion**
- Werewolf Lupin (often treated as **Social Dynamics** or **Dark Arts** depending on context).
- Animagi (humans turning into animals) -> **Magic**.
- Dementors (usually **Threats** due to their nature).

**Edge cases**
- "Follow the spiders": A clue -> **Strategy**. But explicitly about spiders -> **Magical Creatures** (Priority to creature if the focus is the animal).

**Examples from data**

- "He's my rat, sir. He's not really a rat." (Ron)
- "I believe we- we want our gamekeeper back." (Dumbledore - context of Hagrid and his creatures)

### 7. Mentoring
**Definition:** Dialogue offering moral guidance, philosophical wisdom, life lessons, or deep insight that transcends standard academic instruction or simple emotional support.

**Rules for inclusion**
- Moral aphorisms or proverbs (e.g., regarding choices, love, death, or truth).
- Guidance through a moral dilemma or ethical crisis.
- Retrospective wisdom or "teaching moments" about life rather than magic.
- Warnings based on wisdom/experience rather than immediate physical danger.

**Rules for exclusion**
- Teaching a specific spell or fact (Magic).
- Explaining a school rule (Hogwarts Academics).
- Tactical instructions for a mission (Strategy).
- Simple emotional comfort like "It's okay" (Social Dynamics).

**Edge cases**
- "Trust your heart" vs. "Trust me": "Trust your heart" is abstract advice -> Mentoring. "Trust me" is a relational plea -> Social Dynamics.
- Dumbledore explaining Voldemort's past: If focusing on facts for the mission -> Strategy. If focusing on why Voldemort became evil (the lesson) -> Mentoring.

**Examples from data**
- "It does not do to dwell on dreams and forget to live." (Dumbledore)
- "Happiness can be found, even in the darkest of times, if one only remembers to turn on the light." (Dumbledore)
- "Fear of a name increases fear of the thing itself." (Hermione - rare non-Dumbledore example)
